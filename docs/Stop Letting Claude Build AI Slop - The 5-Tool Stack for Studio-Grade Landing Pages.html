The 5 tools Claude needs

Nothing else. The gap between AI template and studio-grade is just these five working together.
Claude Code. The brain. Wires everything else and writes the site.
Nano Banana. Google's image model, runs from your terminal. Real product photography instead of AI art or stock photos. Get an API key from Google AI Studio. The free tier covers your first dozen images.

How the framework works

Don't ask Claude to "build me a landing page." That prompt is what gets you the purple template. Walk it through these 7 steps, in this order.
1. Show it what good looks like. Send 3–5 reference URLs of sites you love. Linear, Stripe, Apple's Vision Pro page, Igloo Inc, anything off awwwards.com. Tell Claude to study them before it writes a single line.
2. Lock the look before any code. Colors, fonts, vibe. Write them down once. Every section has to obey this. No drift halfway through.
3. Plan sections like a magazine. Hero, problem, feature deep-dive, comparison, pricing, closing CTA. Decide the order first. Give each section a different visual treatment so the page doesn't feel monotonous.
4. Pick how things move. Use the named patterns Claude actually knows:
pinned-scrub
,
sticky-stack
,
image-sequence-scrub
,
horizontal-on-vertical
,

. Naming them makes Claude pull from training data instead of guessing.
5. Pull in real parts. Tell Claude to use 21st Dev components and GreenSock patterns. Don't let it write custom ones unless it has to.
6. Generate the imagery. Nano Banana for the hero. Real product photography beats AI illustrations every time.
7. Test, fix, repeat. Screenshot after every section. Compare to your references. Tell Claude what's off. Repeat until your screenshot belongs on Awwwards.

Keywords that unlock Claude

Drop these into your prompts. Each one corresponds to a real pattern Claude already knows.
pinned-scrub — page locks while content animates (Apple Vision Pro hero)
sticky-stack — cards hold while the next slides over (Stripe pricing)
image-sequence-scrub — frame-by-frame product orbit driven by scroll (Apple AirPods)
horizontal-on-vertical — you scroll down, content moves sideways (Linear features)
splittext-reveal — letters animate in one by one (most Stripe heroes)

One pro tip

Don't use pure black for the background. Use
#121414
.
Pure
#000
creates an OLED dead-zone. Your shadows, gradients, and tonal layers have nowhere to live. The whole site looks flat. The slight warm bias of
#121414
is what separates premium dark mode from default dark mode. Same logic for text:
#e3e2e2
, not pure white.
One swap. Big difference.
